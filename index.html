Correlations Discovered Between Various Health Risks and
Age, Median Income, and Poverty


This data was pulled from the US Census Bureau 1 year estimates in 2014, so a further and more robust analysis would include more recent years. However, it is still valuable information to study, as the trends shown during 2014 are most likely very similar to our current year of 2021.

The variables I thought were the most interesting were lack of healthcare compared to percentage of poverty in the state. This analysis shows Texas being the state with the highest poverty rate as well as highest rates of those lacking healthcare. Massachusetts seems to be the state experiencing the lowest numbers of both these variables.
