Correlations Discovered Between Various Health Risks and
Age, Median Income, and Poverty


The above chart shows the possible correlation between various Health Risks (smoking and obesity) to Age, Median Income, and Poverty in 2014. The US Census Bureau has data available to see the trends for 2014, but more data over the recent years are important to see if there's an increase, decrease, or remain somewhat equal in numbers to recent years up to now in 2021.

According to this data, Texas is the state with the worst poverty level and lack of healthcare, which is interesting because I initially thought it was Portland or Seattle for the worst poverty level but maybe the lack of 2021 data makes this misleading to current times. Michigan appears to be the state with the lowest lack of healthcare compared to any other state but there is still a decent amount of poverty. New Hampshire appears to have the lowest poverty rate but still some lack of healthcare, which is interesting.
